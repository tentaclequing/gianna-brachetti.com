TIL that Android will become less open - and for me, this means less secure.

Forgive me for being, once again, late to the party: I have been exploring how to harden my home's security a bit to reduce the footprint we leave that's used in ad networks and the data we might accidentally leak, having seen one too many examples of where they sold our data, or did not keep it secure enough so our data was exposed to bad actors. When I intended to install a more updated KeePass client for my password manager via F-Droid, that's when I saw the notice of what's going to happen.

We should not take this lightly. It really means that our access to more secure options - open-source, free apps that track you less and won't sell your data - will be very limited or even cut off entirely. Not cool.

So I decided to use their banner, and I would warmly but urgently encourage you to [do the same](https://keepandroidopen.org/banner/) and read what else you can do so we might prevent this from happening: https://keepandroidopen.org/cta/

We should have a choice in what happens with our data (and full transparency). This will reduce that choice because it reduces what's available to us.
